# Module 0 — Exam Overview, Study Plan, and Trainer Delivery Plan

> Source: Official CCAO-F Exam Guide, Version 1.0, Effective July 2026 (download the official Exam Guide from the Anthropic Partner Academy). This module summarizes the guide; when in doubt, the guide itself is authoritative and is subject to change without notice.

## 1. What the credential validates

The Claude Certified Associate – Foundations certification validates that a person can apply Claude to complete business and productivity tasks with minimal guidance: using built-in platform features and tools to streamline workflows, identifying process-improvement opportunities, balancing quality/efficiency/cost, and recognizing limitations and escalating complex or technical work to Claude Architects and Developers.

**Audience:** professionals in operations, marketing, project management, education, communications, and general knowledge work — between casual prompt users and technical AI practitioners. **Not** for developers building against APIs or designing agentic systems (that's the Claude Architect / Claude Developer credentials).

**Minimally Qualified Candidate (MQC):** regular hands-on Claude use in a professional setting; foundational applied knowledge of prompt structuring, task orchestration, Projects, Artifacts, and workflow-based interactions; awareness of adoption risks and responsible-AI practices. No prerequisites or required courses — the credential is awarded on exam performance alone.

## 3. Content blueprint (memorize this)

| # | Domain | Weight | ≈ Items of 60 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Prompting and Task Execution | 14% | ~8 |
| 2 | Output Evaluation and Validation | **21%** | ~13 |
| 3 | Product and Model Selection | 12% | ~7 |
| 4 | Workflow Integration and Solution Design | **16%** | ~10 |
| 5 | Configuration and Knowledge Management | 12% | ~7 |
| 6 | Governance, Risk, and Responsible Use | **15%** | ~9 |
| 7 | Troubleshooting and Optimization | 10% | ~6 |

Domains 2, 4, and 6 together are 52% of the exam. Prioritize accordingly.

## 4. Scoring, in plain terms

Criterion-referenced: candidates are measured against a fixed standard set by subject-matter experts, not ranked against each other. The scaled score (100–1,000, cut at 720) is **not** a raw percentage — do not tell learners "you need exactly 72% correct." Domain-level percentages on the score report are diagnostic only; only the total scaled score decides pass/fail.

## 5. Key policies to brief your cohort on

- **Registration:** via Anthropic Partner Academy → Pearson VUE account → schedule (online proctored or test center). Cancel/reschedule up to 24 hours before; inside 24 hours the fee is forfeited.
- **ID:** valid, unexpired government photo ID; name must exactly match registration. Name corrections: certifications-support@anthropic.com before scheduling.
- **Accommodations:** must be requested and approved via Pearson VUE **before** scheduling.
- **Retakes:** waiting periods of 14 days (after 1st fail), 30 days (2nd), 90 days (3rd); max 4 attempts in a rolling 12 months; fee applies each attempt.
- **Conduct:** clear workspace, stay on webcam, no communication, no capturing exam content. Prohibited: phones, smart watches, headphones, study materials, recording devices. Misconduct can invalidate results and ban future exams.
- **NDA:** mandatory before starting; declining ends the session with no refund. **As a trainer, never ask past candidates to share exam items, and never present practice items as real ones.**
- **Renewal:** free, non-proctored renewal assessment on Partner Academy if done on time; a lapsed credential requires a full retake at full fee. Major content changes may require a full retake.
- **Appeals:** within 14 days of notification (or of the exam date for result concerns), via Pearson VUE support. Item content and the standard-setting outcome are not appealable.

## 6. Four-week learner study plan

Assumes ~5–7 hours/week alongside a job. Compress to 2 weeks for experienced daily Claude users.

**Week 1 — Foundations and prompting.** Read the official exam guide end-to-end. Module 0 + Module 1 (Prompting) + Module 3 (Product and Model Selection). Hands-on: complete 3 real work tasks in Claude using structured prompts; deliberately iterate each at least twice.

**Week 2 — The heavyweight domains.** Module 2 (Output Evaluation, 21%) + Module 4 (Workflow Integration, 16%). Hands-on: take one real workflow, redesign it with Claude, and fact-check every factual claim in one Claude output against primary sources.

**Week 3 — Configuration and governance.** Module 5 (Projects/knowledge/connectors) + Module 6 (Governance and responsible use). Hands-on: build a Claude Project with custom instructions and knowledge files for a real use case; write a one-page "appropriate use" note for your team.

**Week 4 — Troubleshooting and rehearsal.** Module 7 + all per-module practice questions + the 60-question mock exam under timed, closed-book conditions. Review every wrong answer against the module content. Re-drill weak domains.

## 7. Trainer delivery plan (7 sessions × ~2 hours)

| Session | Content | In-class activity |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Module 0 + Module 1 | Prompt rewrite workshop: fix 5 weak prompts as a group |
| 2 | Module 2 (part 1: hallucinations, evaluation) | Spot-the-hallucination exercise on prepared outputs |
| 3 | Module 2 (part 2: validation, formats) + Module 3 | Model/feature selection scenario cards |
| 4 | Module 4 | Teams redesign a real workflow from their jobs with Claude |
| 5 | Module 5 | Live-build a Project: instructions + knowledge + connector discussion |
| 6 | Module 6 + Module 7 | Governance case debates; troubleshooting clinic on failing prompts |
| 7 | Timed mock exam (120 min) | Group review of answers by domain; individual gap plans |

Trainer tips: every session should include hands-on Claude time — the exam targets applied judgment, not recall. Use the "minimally qualified candidate" framing when learners over-index on advanced topics: the exam tests sound day-to-day judgment, not API or architecture knowledge. Collect wrong answers from practice questions across the cohort to find shared misconceptions before exam day.

## 8. Official preparation guidance (from the exam guide)

There is no required course, and no resource guarantees a pass. The guide recommends: study the blueprint and self-assess per objective; review official Anthropic documentation and help articles (Projects, Artifacts, Memory, Skills, Code Execution); practice structuring, decomposing, and iterating prompts; build real workflows (configure a Project, evaluate outputs for accuracy and bias); practice responsible-use judgment; and work through the sample questions in guide Section 8.
